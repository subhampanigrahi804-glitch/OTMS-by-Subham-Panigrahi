Main menu:
Anna (Rice) - It is plain steamed rice cooked in earthen pots using traditional firewood method and offered to Lord Jagannath as Mahaprasad.
Dalma - It is a nutritious dish made with lentils cooked along with mixed vegetables like pumpkin, raw banana, brinjal and seasoned mildly without onion or garlic.
Saga Bhaja - It is stir-fried leafy greens such as spinach or amaranth cooked simply with mustard oil and spices.
Mahura - It is mixed vegetables cooked with a unique spice blend used only in the Jagannath Temple kitchen.
Khatta - It is a sweet and tangy preparation made with tamarind, jaggery and fruits like mango or tomato.
Khiri - It is a sweet dish made of rice cooked in milk and jaggery, flavored lightly with cardamom and offered as sacred dessert.

This is a typical menu of the sacred vegetarian Mahaprasad served at Jagannath Temple, Puri.

Description


Jagannath Puri is a coastal town in the Puri district of the Indian state of Odisha. Located along the Bay of Bengal in the eastern part of the state, Puri is one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in India. It is famous for the **Jagannath Temple**, a major Hindu pilgrimage site and one of the four sacred *Char Dham* destinations.

Jagannath Puri is deeply rooted in Hindu religious traditions and is closely associated with Vaishnavism. The Jagannath Temple, dedicated to Lord Jagannath (a form of Lord Vishnu), was built in the 12th century by King Anantavarman Chodaganga Deva of the Eastern Ganga dynasty. The town is renowned for the annual **Rath Yatra (Chariot Festival)**, during which the deities are ceremoniously taken through the streets, attracting millions of devotees. Over centuries, Puri has remained an important center of religious, cultural, and spiritual life in eastern India.
